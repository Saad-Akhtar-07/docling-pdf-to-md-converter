You are segmenting a lecture slide deck into coherent thematic learning units for a tutoring system.

You will be given every slide in the deck, in order, as:

```
Slide <N>: <title>
<source text for this slide>
[AI-generated visual notes, not verbatim source — for context only]: <text, if present>
```

Task: group ALL slides into 6-15 thematic units. Each unit must have:
- `title`: a short, specific title for the unit's topic (not a slide title copy).
- `summary`: one or two sentences describing what the unit covers.
- `slide_ids`: the exact slide numbers belonging to this unit.

Hard rules — a violation makes the output unusable, so follow these exactly:
- Every slide number in the deck must appear in exactly one unit's `slide_ids`. No slide may be omitted. No slide may appear in more than one unit.
- Do not invent slide numbers that were not given to you.
- Units should be contiguous or near-contiguous groups of slides that share one coherent topic — do not scatter one topic's slides across multiple units just to hit a target count.
- Aim for the deck's natural topic boundaries first; a target unit count (if given below) is guidance, not a hard constraint — never violate the slide-partition rule to hit it.

"[AI-generated visual notes]" text describes a slide's image/diagram content as produced by a vision model. It may inform a unit's title or summary wording, but it is not verbatim source text — never treat it as more authoritative than the slide's actual text.
